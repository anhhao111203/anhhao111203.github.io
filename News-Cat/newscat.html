WHAT MAKES A NUTRITIONAL CAT FOOD?

Nutrients are components of the diet that perform specific jobs in the body. Cats can get the nutrients they need from a variety of ingredients. For example, calcium (a nutrient) can come from ingredients like bone, bone meal, dairy products, organ tissues, meat, legume plants, and a mineral supplement. Energy is not technically a nutrient, but it is still an important part of a catâ€™s diet. Dietary energy, measured in kilocalories (also called calories), comes from fats, proteins, and carbohydrates. Nutrients fall into one of six categories:
  • Protein
  • Fat
  • Carbohydrate
  • Vitamin
  • Mineral
  • Water
